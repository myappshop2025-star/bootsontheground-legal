Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 9, 2026

Legal hub: bootsontheground-legal · Client terms: Client Terms of Service

This Privacy Policy describes how Boots on the Ground Services LLC (“BOTG,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and retains information when you use the Boots on the Ground mobile application and related services (the “Service”).

Contact: info@bootsontheground.services. Mailing address: 9169 W State St #2858, Garden City, ID 83714, United States.

Information we collect may include: account and profile details you provide (such as name, email, phone number, role, company name, service area, travel radius, and whether you are available for new jobs as a field agent); property and inspection information you submit (including addresses, descriptions, photos, deadlines, and status updates); payment-related metadata (we do not store full card numbers on our servers—see “Payments” below); device, push-notification token, notification preferences, and usage data needed to operate and secure the Service; explanations you submit when requesting administrative review of a profile restriction; communications you send to support; and records needed for fraud prevention, dispute resolution, and legal compliance.

Payments and payouts: When you add a payment method or pay for jobs or tips, sensitive card and bank data are collected and processed by Stripe, Inc. and its affiliates (“Stripe”) as described at checkout and in Stripe’s privacy policy. We may store limited payment metadata (for example card brand, last four digits, bank mask, Stripe customer IDs, payment method IDs, and charge or payout identifiers) so you can manage saved methods, complete purchases, and view transaction history. If you are a field agent, we use Stripe Connect to move earned amounts to your connected account and, when you withdraw, to send funds to your linked bank. We store payout ledger entries and Stripe payout identifiers to show withdrawal history and status. Bank settlement timing depends on Stripe and your financial institution.

Field agents and fairness: If you cancel an assigned or in-progress job, we may store the cancellation reason you select (which may be visible to the requester on that inspection) and operational data used for in-app fairness rules (for example short-notice cancellation counts by calendar quarter and any temporary restriction that limits accepting new open jobs until it expires).

How we use information: To provide, personalize, and improve the Service; to process payments and payouts; to communicate with you about the Service; to verify accounts where applicable; to maintain safety and integrity; to comply with law; to enforce our Terms; and for internal analytics and operations (including troubleshooting and service reliability).

Sharing: We share information with service providers that help us run the Service, including Google Firebase (authentication, database, storage, and related cloud infrastructure as configured for the app), Stripe (payments and Connect), notification vendors (for example for device push notifications), and other subprocessors we engage for hosting, security, or communications. We may disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to applicable law and any required notice.

Push notifications: If you opt in, we may store device push tokens and related metadata so we can deliver notifications about jobs, payouts, account activity, or similar Service events. You can manage notification permissions in your device settings.

Location: If you grant permission, we may collect precise device location while the app is in use (not continuous background tracking unless separately disclosed). For field agents, this includes: (1) optional “Use my current location” in Browse Jobs to filter and map open jobs near you while that toggle is on—the app may update your position as you move; and (2) navigation and property-related workflows during assigned or in-progress jobs. Matching and accepting open jobs also uses your profile service area and travel radius you set in the app, which may differ from your live location. You can disable location permission in device settings or turn off in-app location features.

Public profiles and availability (field agents): Public profile fields visible to clients (such as bio, expertise, and company name) must not include direct phone numbers or email addresses. We may limit account access until such information is removed. If you believe a restriction is incorrect, you may submit a written explanation through the app for administrative review. You may turn off “Available for new jobs” in Profile to pause appearing in client search, nearby job alerts, and acceptance of new open jobs while you manage existing assignments.

Retention: We retain information for as long as needed to provide the Service and for legitimate business and legal purposes (including tax, accounting, and dispute resolution), unless a longer period is required or permitted by law. When you delete your account, we initiate removal of your data from our systems where feasible and ask Stripe to delete or disconnect billing and Connect resources where Stripe’s APIs allow; Stripe may retain certain records as required by law or Stripe policy.

Security: We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

Your rights: Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or export certain personal information, or to object to or restrict certain processing. To submit a request, email us at the contact address above. We may need to verify your identity before responding. We do not sell your personal information for monetary consideration as that term is commonly understood under U.S. state privacy laws.

Children: The Service is not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If you believe we have collected such information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

International users: If you access the Service from outside the United States, you understand that your information may be processed in the United States and other countries where we or our service providers operate.

Changes: We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised policy in the app and update the “Last updated” date above. Material changes may be communicated as required by law. Continued use of the Service after the effective date of changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy where permitted by law.
